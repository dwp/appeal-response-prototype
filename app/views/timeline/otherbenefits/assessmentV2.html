Example response
Entitlement to PIP is not the same as having limited capability for work. It is a different benefit with different purposes and different assessment criteria.

In this appeal, we can consider the medical evidence which was used to decide PIP entitlement, but not the entitlement itself.

As part of their PIP assessment, the appellant may have shown that they need to use an aid to complete daily activities or to move around.

Because the use of such aids would be reasonable in the modern workplace, and because the appellant could then complete the activity on a reliable and repeated basis, the Work Capability Assessment would not consider this to indicate limited capability for work.
